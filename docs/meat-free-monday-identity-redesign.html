Meat-Free Monday is an existing campaign that makes people think again about the impact of eating meat and recommend  cu of meat one day a week, for the sake of environment, thinking of animals, being healthy, slow down extinction of species, prevent world hunger, saving pennies, etc.

 

This Project is a redesigning project of currently existing campaign, Meat Free Monday.

I focused on how to make the brand more attractive, While at the same time keeping the current brand image by making it feel warm, natural, and free. For specific graphic style, I kept the image of Heart with face and the slab-serif font.

Almost most of the text in this images, and recipes are from the original website, linked own below.

To specify the brand identity, there many modifications of symbol were made. Starting with this, Brand Menual Book was made to give the brand a consistent look and feel.

Advertsement Means -  Album Series (Meat Free Monday With Music)

One of the special story of Meat Free Monday Campaign is that it was launched by Paul Mccartney, the Beatles' former member, and Many other celebrities including singers are supporting the campaign.  It actually makes people interested in the campaign and be familiar.  Therefore, adding on Brand Menual Book, a concept compilation album series was made to privide the campaign a channel to communicate with people.

 

The album cover is  just a single craft A4-sized paper. Its first page has illustration of animals we protect in this campaign, explanation of why this campaign is making this album series, and one of the goals that can be achieved by joying the campaign.  On the back, it has QR code that links to the site, and recipes from the site.

This design of front/back cover makes the album cover work as a poster or as recipe, not only as a cover.

 

The series are made of 3 versions, cheerful, fun and calm songs. Songs are carefully chosen with this concept, that consist of  Paul Mccartney and  MFM supporters'.  In addition, celebrating that this Christmas(2017) is on Monday,  Christmas version was made. Click image to see it large.
